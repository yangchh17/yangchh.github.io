Project 04b

Watershed Delineation &
Hydrological Assessment

LiDAR-derived terrain analysis to map stream networks, delineate watersheds, and model surface drainage patterns — supporting environmental assessment and water resource planning in complex BC terrain.

DomainWater Resources
MethodDEM Hydrology
RoleGIS Specialist

00
Context

Hydrological assessments underpin environmental impact studies, flood mapping, water licence applications, and salmon habitat inventories. Accurate drainage network delineation depends critically on the quality of the underlying terrain model — making LiDAR-derived DEMs far superior to coarse provincial products for site-level hydrology work.

01
Problem

A watershed study in west-central BC required delineating sub-watershed boundaries, mapping stream networks, and characterising flow accumulation patterns to support an environmental assessment. The study area spanned multiple terrain types — steep glacial valleys, low-gradient floodplains, and peatland complexes — each presenting distinct hydrological modelling challenges.

02
Approach
Phase A — DEM Conditioning

Hydrological conditioning applied to the LiDAR-derived DTM using ArcGIS Pro Spatial Analyst — filling sinks, breaching barriers, and burning in known stream centrelines from provincial hydrographic data (BC Freshwater Atlas) to ensure hydrological connectivity.

Phase B — Drainage Analysis

Flow direction computed using D8 algorithm. Flow accumulation raster derived to identify channel initiation points. Stream networks extracted at multiple accumulation thresholds and compared against field-verified channel locations.

Phase C — Watershed Delineation

Pour points defined at downstream outlet locations. Sub-watersheds delineated using the Watershed tool, attributed with area, mean slope, and stream order. Final deliverable: a nested watershed hierarchy GIS dataset with full drainage characterization.
